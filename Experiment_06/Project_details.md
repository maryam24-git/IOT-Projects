#  Basic Electronic Components & Signal Analysis

This lab explores the fundamental relationship between voltage, current, and resistance by building a simple LED circuit. 
The primary objective is to observe how different resistor values affect the current flow and the brightness of an LED using Ohm's Law ($I = V/R$).

## Analysis
The experiment demonstrates **Ohm's Law ($I = V/R$)**. 
As the resistance ($R$) increases while the supply voltage ($V$) remains constant at 5V, the current ($I$) flowing through the circuit decreases. This reduction in current leads to a decrease in the light intensity of the LED.

## Conclusion
This confirms that resistance is inversely proportional to current. Higher resistance limits the electron flow, thereby reducing the brightness of the LED.
